Alan Turing

The greatest hero of World War II, and the “father” of modern day computing.
Alan Turing in 1928 at age 16

Here's a timeline of Alan' life :

  • 1912 - Alan Mathison Turing born in Maida Vale, London, to Ethel Sara Turing (née Stoney) and Julius Mathison Turing.
  • 1927-30 - Alan furthers his love of science and maths by reading Einstein whilst at Sherborne. His great friend, Christopher Marcom, who shares his interests, suddenly dies. Alan is devastated.
  • 1934 - Alan graduates with distinction in Mathematics from King's College, Cambridge.
  • 1938 - Alan goes to Princeton University in America to study mathematics and is awarded a PhD.
  • 1939 - September, Alan is asked to join the Government Codes and Ciphers School and arrives at Bletchley Park the day after war is declared.
  • 1940 -With Gordon Welchman, Alan develops the Bombe to decipher the messages sent by the Germans using their Enigma machine.
  • 1943-45 - Alan works with top people in the USA, which he visits to share information on code breaking.
  • 1945 - At the end of the war, Alan Turing is awarded the OBE for his wartime service.
  • 1946 - Alan publishes a paper with the first detailed design of a stored-program computer.
  • 1949 - Alan is made deputy director of the Computing Laboratory at Manchester University.
  • 1950 - Aland publishes the famous paper 'Computing Machinery and Intelligence' in which he develops the Turing Test.
  • 1951 - Alan is elected Fellow of the Royal Society, and also gives talks about Artificial Intelligence on the BBC.

"His actions saved countless lives. He also left a remarkable national legacy through his substantial scientific achievements, often being referred to as the father of modern computing."

- Prime Minister of the United Kingdom David Cameron
